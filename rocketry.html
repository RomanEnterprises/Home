Saturn V1 Rocket

The Saturn V1 Rocket is a rocket specifically designed for the American Rocket Competition (ARC) to provide high school students with a platform to learn about rocketry and aerospace engineering. It is a single-stage, F32T-6 rocket that is designed to reach 750 feet. The Saturn V1 is designed to be easy to build and launch, making it an ideal choice for students who are new to rocketry. It features a simple design that allows for easy assembly and disassembly, as well as a robust construction that can withstand the stresses of launch and flight. The Saturn V1 Rocket is an excellent tool for teaching students about the principles of rocketry and aerospace engineering, and it provides a fun and exciting way for students to get involved in STEM education. Its main construction is made of 3D printed parts, which allows for modularity and ease of manufacturing. It is a good cheap way to prototype allowing for our club to gain expericence in design whilst keeping costs low. The Saturn V1 is a great example of small scale enginuety and is a stepping stone for the future for more complicated rockets to hopefully one day win the ARC competition. We are planned for launch on March 28th. This rocket operates under STEM club at Saint Louis University High School.
